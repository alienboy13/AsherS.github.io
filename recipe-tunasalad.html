Tuna Salad

Conceived during the COVID quarantine of 2020

Amount of food created: Can create lunches for a week

Costs: $24.08 to make this recipe twice, meaning fourteen meals at a total of around $1.72 per meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2-4 cans of salmon
  • 2-3 slices of a Red Onion
  • 5-7 Baby Dill pickles
  • 4-6 spoonfuls of mayonaise
  • 1 cup of Shredded Carrots
  • Yellow Mustard: Add to taste
  • Taco sauce: Add to taste (I hate I have to say this about taco sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Description:

I think that my mother was the start of this recipe. She taught me how to make tuna salad (she used salmon, that's nurture, not nature), and I went, "Hey, what if I made this taste like something that isn't mustard?" So, I had lunches that were almost exclusively "Tuna Salad" for about a month, and since I had already removed the spice from life, I eventually decided to put taco sauce in my "Tuna Salad" to compensate.

Some "scholars" compare this to a dish called "Herring Under a Fur Coat" that is apparently Russian in origin. Unfortunately, mine doesn't use potatoes or eggs, and instead replaces them with taco sauce. Not exactly true to the source material but I'll take any opportunity to have people stop reacting to the appearance of "Tuna Salad" by gaining the sudden overwhelming urge to destroy it.

Prep

1. Drain and place the salmon in the bowl. Break it up with a fork so it's at the bottom, especially if you're using four cans. If you're using four cans you're sacrificing taste for supply, but due to expiration times you'd have to eat it every day for a couple meals a day to fit it in a week. If so, Godspeed.

2. Slice up the pickles, first longways in half, then take a half, cut it in half, and then while you're holding it down, cut it crossways into tinier pieces. Repeat for each pickle, and move to side with knife.

3. Slice 3-4 cross-sections out of the onion, cut each like a pizza into triangles, but with really thin slices so the section of the furthest out ring is only about the length of the top joint of the thumb. Don't ask questions, just follow the instructions. If you want to cut them differently, I have no way to stop you, but that’s how it was done originally.

4. Now, put the mayonnaise in the bowl, mix it into the salmon until it looks like it's just kind of too much. Don't worry, it is too much, but the other ingredients will fix that.

5. Now, add the mustard and taco sauce right after another. Add to balance the mayonnaise, it should be slightly pinkish once you're done evenly mixing it.

6. Evenly mix in the pickles and the onions. Make sure that there's not any clumps, because that makes it more difficult to make sandwiches, and especially difficult to eat it with crackers.

7. At this point, I'd usually slap it on a sandwich with toasted bread and lettuce (because all my recipes end up as a sandwich, a soup, or a salad) If not, crackers!
