Tuna Salad

Conceived during the COVID quarantine of 2020

Amount of food created: Can create lunches for a week

Costs: $21.08 to make this recipe four times, meaning seven meals at a total of around $2.70 per meal. Quik Maths.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of rice
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas
  • 1 cup of White Onion
  • 1/2 a Roasted Garlic Summer Sausage
  • 1 cup of Shredded Carrots
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1/3 cup of General Tso's Stirfry sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Description:

I think that my mother was the start of this recipe. She taught me how to make tuna salad (she used salmon, that's nurture, not nature), and I went, "Hey, what if I made this taste like something that isn't mustard?" So, I had lunches that were almost exclusively "Tuna Salad" for about a month, and since I had already removed the spice from life, I eventually decided to put taco sauce in my "Tuna Salad" to compensate.

Some "scholars" compare this to a dish called "Herring Under a Fur Coat" that is apparently Russian in origin. Unfortunately, mine doesn't use potatoes or eggs, and instead replaces them with taco sauce. Not exactly true to the source material but I'll take any opportunity to have people stop reacting to the appearance of "Tuna Salad" by gaining the sudden overwhelming urge to destroy it.

Prep

1. Get one full cup of rice (as per the measurement cup in the rice cooker that was in Asher's apartment in late 2023) and rinse it with a colander before you place it in the rice cooker that was in Asher's apartment in late 2023. Then, fill it up to the line with water. If you're using the rice cooker that was in Asher's apartment in 2023, be sure to place it in a bowl of water so that it doesn't overflow. That's important. Start the rice cooker cooking.

If you don't have the rice cooker that was in Asher's apartment in 2023, just make a cup of rice

2. Chop a cup of white onion, and rinse a cup of sugar snap peas and a cup of carrots. Good job, you're eating your vegetables. Also chop up half of a roasted garlic summer sausage. How much is this in volume you ask? I don't care, the more the better.

3. Prepare the liquids and seasonings used later. Combine 1/3 a cup of General Tso's sauce, a tablespoon of turmeric, and a teaspoon of Old Bay.

Cooking Time!!!

WARNING: The rice will be done at some point. I know, very scary. You can let it warm if it's in a rice cooker that auto warms (such as the rice cooker in Asher's apartment in late 2023), but if not you have to deal with it.

1. Add a tablespoon of olive oil to the pan, let it heat, and begin sautéing the vegetables. They normally take about seven minutes. I've been told not to cook like a scientist, so just go until you think that they're almost there.

2. Next, add the sliced roasted garlic summer sausage. You should attempt to brown this for about two more minutes, really watching the vegetables more than the meat. If you can, press the sausage into the pan to attempt to brown it more on the edges.

3. Now, add the liquid mixture that I like to call "Asher's Stirfry sauce". It's sort of like Asher's constant (1240 ev), but it's way cooler. Cook this for a couple more minutes, and then you can pour out into a mixing bowl.

(Preferably somewhere where you don't delaminate a table.)

4. Pour the rice over the stirfry in the mixing bowl, and then mix them together with a mixing spoon, possibly the same one as used to stir in the stirfry.

5. Enjoy! This makes about two meals, so feel free to share with a friend or enjoy leftovers.
